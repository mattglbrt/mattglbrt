This is the 33rd January of my life, and this one feels different. I'm starting this site to document my year. This is the fourth year of my business, Everyway LLC, existing. I started Everway in 2019, right before Covid hit. For the first couple of years, Everyway functioned as a side hustle and didn't bring in a ton of money in relation to my day job. I didn't have a website, I was not actively marketing it, all my clients came through word of mouth referrals, and it stayed this way up until 2022.

In January 2022, I was unexpectedly fired from my high-paying day job, weeks before I would have received a substantial amount of vested shares. This hit me hard for many reasons. I had been working 12-14 hour days for months to the detriment of both my health and family. The amount of money I was expecting to receive from the vested shares would have been life-changing. My wife was pregnant and due to give birth in March. 30 days before this happened, my house flooded, wiping out our savings to deal with the repairs and clean-up. So, on January 14th, 2022, I was left with $0 in savings and what remained of my final paycheck. Luckily, I did receive a "severance package," which was just my Q4 bonus that I had already earned. This gave me another month of runway, but we would run out of money right before the new baby arrived.

I spent the rest of that Friday afternoon crying. I had felt deeply committed to the team and business, I was pouring myself into it every day, I was getting results, I trusted the leaders of the organization. Losing out on the money was a minor part of what happened to me during this time. I was disillusioned by many things about corporate culture, publicly traded companies, and just humans in general. This was a paradigm-shifting event for me.
Then I spent the rest of the weekend not thinking about our impending financial doom; I was entirely present with my wife and daughter, refilling my bucket and remembering why I was working so hard in the first place.

Never again.

I realized that no matter what I did, I was going to throw 100% of myself into it. I am not a person capable of "quiet quitting" as it's called these days. No disrespect to people who are; I get it. It is just not who I am. I am someone who wants to get involved in everything, be a force of change. I commit 100% or 0%. I am not a person of moderation when it comes to anything. Acknowledging this, I realized that I could never work FOR someone else again. The only person I could give 100% of myself to fairly, honestly, and purely was myself. I love working WITH people, but not for them. After what happened to me throughout 2021 and the culmination in 2022, I could never put my trust or security in the hands of someone else. Period. I had to be my own source of financial security.

At that time we did not have financial security in terms of cash in the bank, but we had and still do have security due to my ability to create wealth. The nature of my career has always been making other people money, building other people's dreams. This is the part I love about what I do. I had never made a real solid attempt at doing it for myself.

After getting burned, I spent Sunday night reflecting and really trying to figure out what I wanted to do; that was a process that would take all year, and really only solidify as I am writing this. I decided that I would much rather empower ordinary people to go from $0 to their first Million, something only 10% of small businesses reach, and further those businesses to 10m. These small businesses are the lifeblood of local economies. They provide meaningful employment to their communities and can newly create generational wealth. Uplifting real people.

It was this decision that I brought to my pregnant wife, "Hey babe, we run out of money in about 45 days. Also, I am not going to look for a job either."

She just replied, "Ok cool. I have faith in you; I'm not worried about it."

I woke up at 3 am the following Monday. Something that I had been doing out of necessity for my previous employer for months, but that was the first day I did it for myself. I wrote a few long-form posts similar to this one, threw it out on Facebook, Twitter, and LinkedIn to my current network. At the time I had even less of a following than I do right now, I didn't have a website. I think my LinkedIn profile was mostly incomplete. I also sent out a few emails to some previous colleagues that I was going out on my own and looking for some opportunities. From a few social media posts and tapping my current network, I closed two $3000 client retainers and a $10,000 one-time project in the first week of deciding to make Everyway my full-time pursuit.

Just like that, if we budgeted tight, we could make it July 1st. I could pay COBRA insurance to cover the birth of my son. We were making it.

June comes, and although I have secured a few more minor projects, it becomes clear to me that my current network is tapped for the time being and I need to start doing something to bring in net new clients. I throw up a landing page describing a few of the services I can offer and start cold-emailing businesses that had job postings available for what I could and tried to turn them into clients instead. Right around June from doing this, I was referred to a company called Invisible Commerce, with this contract, we were set. The owners of Invisible Commerce conducted themselves with honesty, transparency, and a work ethic that refilled me, gave me hope that not all corporate or start-up culture was the same. Also, in July, the original organization that fired me in January realized they needed me to come back and work directly with some of the stakeholders that I still had trust and faith in. I accepted the contract and worked on some specific projects that I loved and could dump the passion and work ethic into.

With these two large contracts in place, I continued to expand my network and pick up more and more smaller projects and contracts. Still with no website and no real business strategy on my end. Despite that being the very service I was offering to my clients. I kept going wide and doing more and more things, stretching myself thinner and thinner. I had done all that I could for Invisible Commerce after a year, and at this time, the other organization went through a substantial downsizing in July as well, no longer funding the project team I was consulting on. So this left me in July 2023, with small contracts but nothing that would cover the bills, but this time we had established a nest egg for both the business operations and our personal overhead.

Again I came to my wife, "Hey just ended both major contracts, and I hate what I have been doing for the last 10 years, going to take a break and reset and then hit it hard again."

And again, "I have faith in you."

I spent the last 6 months soul-searching, planning, meaningfully resting, and of course signing new clients and projects to keep the lights on, but I haven't been building Everyway as a business like how I should be. I realized that what I have been lacking for essentially my entire life is a real Vision, a Mission. My mission has always been to make as much money as possible. That is no mission, and a terrible vision.

So here I am, January 14th is coming up again, the 2-year anniversary of trying to build this business into something. This year I am going to take the business seriously, myself seriously, and build this thing, and do my best to document it here, in the hopes of helping as many people as I can do the same, and of course sign some of you as clients along the way.

Oh, what's my vision? If you made it this far, you can check it out on post number 2.
