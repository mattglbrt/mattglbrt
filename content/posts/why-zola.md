# Why Zola?

I wish I could tell you elaborate and deeply technical reasons why I picked Zola instead of Astro, Next, Gatsby, or any of the myriad other options, but that’s not really why I picked it.

The reason why goes back to Christmas 2022. Every year my wife and I pick out books for each other. One of my books was [Billy Summers by Steven King] (https://amzn.to/3IcKDRC). In this book, the main character is reading the novel, [Thérèse Raquin](https://amzn.to/3Ip9WzM) . At the time, I had no idea what that was, but a few months later, while checking the book section at my local thrift store, something I do weekly. I happen to come across this very novel. Written by Emile Zola. Of course, I bought it.

Later that same day, I joined the JavaScript Jam Twitter space, and Zola came up in the open mic discussion.

I have learned to embrace the synchronicities in my life and immediately ran Zola init, and here we are. 

Ok, for real, after checking the [homepage](https://www.getzola.org) out, I realized that Zola was exactly what I had been looking for. I have been wanting to build a series blog using an ssg, but I kept getting lost in the weeds trying to build the front end and getting sucked in by various bells and whistles of other static site generators and would end up never actually getting to the blogging part.

## Elaborate and Deeply Technical Reasons
Zola is one dependency that does everything I wanted in a blog:
* **Static Site Generation** - I think most websites are bloated and filled with garbage these days. I wanted SSG and the site to be as fast and minimal as possible.
* **No dependencies** - I have been burned so many times by using plugins with Gatsby (which is totally my fault) that I just wanted it to work out of the box. Zola has syntax highlighting, read times, and a table of contents. That’s all I really needed.
* **Markdown** - The goat. I a not sure when I started using markdown, but at this point, I think in markdown.

I am pushing the current site live on January 1st, 2023. A fresh start. 

This site’s design is perfect for what I am doing now, going back to basics and minimizing everything as much as possible. I am using the [sam theme](https://www.getzola.org/themes/sam/) by Jan Baudisch.

Over the last couple of months, I let my life and systems get convoluted. I am currently cutting my dependency on technology, tools, apps, and platforms back as much as possible and instead working to build discipline within.

Losing my primary source of income in January 2022 with a pregnant wife due in March and nothing in the bank account really changed something in me.

I have been running a race since last January and only recently have been able to catch my breath. Coming up on the 1 year anniversary of going out on my own and breaking free of the matrix, I am reflecting on the last year. While it was the most financially successful year of my life. It was by far the most stressful. Now I am ready to start the next leg of the race to create sustainable growth and share what I have learned over the last 12 years of being in the development, marketing, and sales industries.
