# A Fresh Start
I think today is the day I will push this live and get it out into the world. I have lost count of the amount of times I have tried to start a personal blog and get it out there. I kept getting caught up on things that didn't matter, so I made this extremely minimal site that has the parts that I didn’t get caught up on. 

This site’s design is perfect for what I am doing in my life now, going back to basics and minimizing everything as much as I can. Over the last couple of months, I let my life and systems get convoluted. I am currently cutting my dependency on technology, tools, apps, and platforms back as much as possible. 

In the past, I had the intention of creating an entrepreneurial focused blog, but I realize now that my procrastination has stemmed from resistance because doing that and eventually selling courses never sat right with me. Don’t get me wrong, I have created several courses in the past, and even taught them in person in the before-times, all for free. Riding the wave of pimping a course that should have really just been a blog post was never what I wanted to do.  Not taking anything away from the people that are currently doing it. They have much more discipline and focus than I do. Even now, as I am writing this, I am not sure if marketing or entrepreneurship is even what I want to write about in the first place. I’m not sure if it is even what I would like to be doing at all. This last year has been the most transformative year of my life. Losing my job in January with a pregnant wife due in March really did something to me.  I feel like I have been running a race since January and really only recently have been able to catch my breath. I want this period to be a resting area that I can continue the climb from and not a plateau. I have given myself til January to regroup and figure things out for the next leg of the race. 

*Is 32 too late to ask myself what I want to do with my life?*

Have I created this site just to publicly stumble through trying to find out? So much of my life has revolved around “work” and mostly work for other people, that I have no idea what I actually enjoy anymore. I know that I enjoy creating. Still vague, but a little more specific, I enjoy three things, writing, reading, and painting. My goal for 2023 is to create a path forward that is focused on these 3 things. I want to create multiple revenue streams around the things that I am already doing. I want to remove a work/life balance and just live. Is it possible to generate enough money to live by living alone? Does the act of generating revenue from an activity change the activity itself? What happens when making money is a byproduct and not the main goal? Will I be able to generate enough of it? What is enough? Why am I sitting here writing this instead of doing actual work in the first place?

With that stream of consciousness ramble over. My goals for this month are to finish up some loose threads that have been dangling for years. All conventional wisdom says to pick one thing and focus on it. Conventional wisdom hasn’t worked for me in the past, and I am not going to let it hold me back going into the future. I want to do a lot of things, so a lot of things I will do.

- [ ] Mattglbrt.com (this site) Live
- [ ] Mdggrowth.com finished and live
- [ ] FreeTimeProfit.com finished and live
- [ ] The Hobbinomicon Started
- [ ] Busy Playn Booth Set Up and Ready for Events
- [ ] Sign up for NaNoWriMo and plan my first novel
- [ ] Build goeveryway.com and launch

The to-do list above touches on everything I want to pursue in earnest next year. I have my sites set on next year already because I have smashed all my goals for this year already. Here is a brief overview of my upcoming projects.

### MDG Growth
This is my parent LLC that owns my commerce stores. We currently only have busyplayn.com operational, but I want to relaunch inkhide and start a few more brands next year. By having this site ready to go I will have an anchor for everything else, and it has been sitting at about 80% finished for nearly 3 years now. 

### Free Time Profit
I realized that maybe I do want to have that entrepreneurial outlet, but I want to focus on beginners and help people help themselves. I have a few ideas that I think will be cool, and I do not necessarily want my personal site focused on that.

### The Hobbinomicon
Throughout my life there has been one constant – miniature painting, modeling, and tabletop games. I have been longing to be involved in this niche at a large scale, and the Hobbinomicon is my way of contributing back into a community from which I have gained so much. 

### Busy Playn
What started as a fun passion project for my wife and I, has taken off, and now I see the potential to turn this into a million dollar brand. We have recently finished up our soft launch and with this data, I will begin to move into full scale operations and marketing. For now, we have booked several vendor booths and events so that is my immediate priority.

### NaNoWriMo
I have wanted to write a book since I was a kid. So, I am jumping in and doing it. I am using NaNoWriMo to stay motivated and get it done. By the end of November I will have a finished novel. What it will be about, I currently have no idea. 

### Everyway
My consulting business has become my main source of income over this last year, and it really shows that with a proper offer and networking you don’t need a website or any other marketing. I have been consulting as a jack of all trades, and recently realized that this has been holding me back. My goal for everyway going into the next year is to niche down and start focus on specific solutions to a specific audience. 

My plan for the month is ridiculous. I am overcommitting and underestimating the amount of things I will be able to get done in the next 2 weeks, but it is fun to dream, and I will finish a good chunk of this list this month. The important thing is to not get mad at myself if I do not finish it all because no matter what I will be closer to my goals anyway. I am not someone that can sit and focus one project at a time. I never have been and trying to force myself to do it doesn’t work. I used to think this was because I lacked discipline, and I have spent years mistakenly trying to build discipline in myself. Only recently I realized I have the discipline, I just need to play into my strengths. I like having multiple challenges, I like spreading myself thin, I like bouncing around from different projects in a single day. If I tried to stick to one, I would procrastinate and work so slowly on it, it would never launch anyway. 

Going forward on this line of thinking, I will detail my progress towards this list and how I manage to stay organized and productive. Hopefully.
