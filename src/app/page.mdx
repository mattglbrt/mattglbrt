## Spiraling Upward {{ date: '2024-01-31T00:00Z' }}

This is the 33rd January of my life, and it feels different. My business, Everyway LLC, is about to be four years old. I started Everway legally speaking, right before Covid hit in early March of 2020. For the first two years, Everyway functioned as a side hustle and didn't bring in a ton of money compared to my day job. I didn't have a website, I was not actively marketing it, all my clients came through word of mouth, and it stayed this way until 2022.

On January 14, 2022, I was unexpectedly laid off from my high-paying day job.

I had been working 12-14 hour days for months to the detriment of both my health and family. I was putting these hours in because in just a few more weeks, I would have received an amount of vested shares that would have been life-changing. To top things off 30 days earlier, my house flooded, wiping out our savings to deal with the repairs and clean-up, and my wife was pregnant and due to give birth to our second child in March.

So, on January 14th, 2022, I was left with $0 in savings and what remained of my final paycheck. Luckily, I did receive a "severance package," just my Q4 bonus that I had already earned. Definitely not still bitter. This gave me another month of runway, but we would run out of money right before the new baby arrived.

I spent the rest of that Friday afternoon crying. I had felt deeply committed to the team and company. I was pouring myself into it every day. I was getting results. I trusted the leaders of the organization, mostly. Losing out on the money was a minor part of what upset me.

## This was a paradigm-shifting event for me. 

I spent the rest of the weekend not thinking about our impending financial doom. Instead, I was entirely present with my wife and daughter, refilling my bucket and remembering why I was working so hard in the first place.
I realized that no matter what I did, I would throw 100% of myself into it. I am not a person capable of "quiet quitting" as it's called these days. No disrespect to people who are; I get the appeal. It is just not who I am. I am someone who puts a hand in every cookie jar I can find. I strive to be a force of change.

I commit 100% or 0%. I am not a person of moderation when it comes to anything. Acknowledging this about myself, I realized the only person I could give 100% of myself to fairly and honestly was myself. I had to be my own source of security.

The nature of my career has always been building other people's dreams. This is the part I love about what I do, but I had never made a real attempt at doing it for myself. I spent that Sunday night reflecting and trying to figure out what I wanted to do with my life. This process has taken the last two years and really only solidified recently.

I wanted to empower ordinary people to go from $0 to their first Million, something only 10% of small businesses reach, and then help expand 1m businesses to 10m.

These small businesses are the lifeblood of local economies. They provide meaningful employment to their communities and can create generational wealth for everyday people.

It was this decision that I brought to my pregnant wife, "Hey babe, we run out of money in about 45 days. Also, I am not going to look for a job."
She just replied, "Ok cool. I have faith in you. I'm not worried about it."

I woke up at 3am the following Monday. Something that I had been doing out of necessity for my previous employer for months, but that was the first day I did it for myself. I wrote a few long-form posts similar to this one and threw it out on Facebook, Twitter, and LinkedIn.

At the time I had even less of a following than I do right now, I didn't have a website. I think my LinkedIn profile was mostly incomplete. I also sent out some emails to colleagues mentioning that I was going out on my own and looking for opportunities. From a few social media posts and tapping my current network, I closed two $3000 client retainers and a $10,000 one-time project in the first week of deciding to make Everyway my full-time pursuit.

Just like that, if we budgeted tight, we could make it July 1st. I could pay COBRA insurance to cover the birth of my son. We were making it.
June comes, and although I have secured a few additional projects, it becomes clear that my current network is tapped for the time being and I need to start doing something to bring in new clients.

## Here is an actual tip for people that are looking to start freelancing or their own business:

I threw up a landing page describing a few of the services I could offer and started cold-emailing businesses that had job postings available for roles similar to my service offerings.

Around June, I was referred to a company called Invisible Commerce, with this contract, we were set. The owners of Invisible Commerce conducted themselves with honesty, transparency, and a work ethic that refilled me. Also, in July, the original company that fired me in January asked me to come back and work directly with some of the business leaders that I still had (and continue to have) respect for.

With these two large contracts in place, I continued to expand my network and pick up more and more contracts. Still with no website and no real business strategy on my end. Despite that being the very service I was offering to my clients. I kept going wide and doing more and more things, stretching myself thinner and thinner.

I had done all that I could for Invisible Commerce after a year, and at this same time, the other company went through a substantial downsizing, no longer funding the team I was consulting. This left me back at ground 0 in July 2023, but this time we had established a proper savings account.
Again I came to my wife, "Hey just ended both major contracts, and I am burnt out. Going to take a break, reset, and then hit it hard again."
And again, "I have faith in you."

I've written this story down for the first time mostly for myself to have a frame of reference for where I started. I spent the last 6 months soul-searching, planning, meaningfully resting, and of course signing new clients and projects to keep the lights on, but I haven't been building Everyway as a business like how I should be. What I have been missing for essentially my entire life is a real Vision, a Mission, a defining purpose. My mission has always been to make as much money as possible. That is not a mission, that is not a true purpose.

So here I am, January 14th came and went recently, and surprise, my wife is once again pregnant, this time with twins. Two years later, and in many ways, I am back to where I started with just as much or maybe more pressure to succeed.
