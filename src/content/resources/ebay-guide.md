# **Best Practices for Starting on eBay (The System That Actually Works)**

Most people make eBay harder than it needs to be. They chase every random item they find, price things too low, list inconsistently, and wonder why their store never grows. The truth is that eBay only rewards two things: **consistency** and **smart math**. If you get those two pieces right, everything else becomes a lot easier.

After years of selling online, here’s the framework I use and the same one I recommend to anyone who wants to build a real, profitable eBay side business.

---

## **1. Your Average Selling Price Needs To Be $50**

This is the first big rule.  
Your **Average Sell Price (ASP)** should hit **$50**. Not every item needs to be $50 individually, but your *monthly average* needs to land there.

You can balance a $25 item with a $75 item.  
You can bundle two $10 items with a $30 item.  
You can pair a $40 item with a $60 item.

But at the end of the month, when you divide total sales by total items sold, that number needs to come out to around $50.

Why?  
Because below $50, it’s extremely hard to consistently hit a **40% profit margin**, which is the margin you want if you’re doing this seriously.

There’s too much friction under $20 fees, shipping costs, time spent sourcing, and the mental load of selling low value items.  
That’s why I say this plainly:

**Do not sell items under $20.**  
If you have to, bundle them until you get the value up.

If you build your store on $12 sales, you’ve already lost.

---

## **2. You Should Aim for a 50% Monthly Sell-Through Rate**

This is the metric almost nobody tracks, and it tells you whether your store is truly healthy.

Your **Sell-Through Rate (STR)** is the percentage of your active listings that sell in a given month.

A sustainable eBay store should land around **50% sell-through every month**.

Here’s how to think about it:

- If you have 300 active listings, you should be selling ~150 per month.  
- Below 30%? You're buying the wrong inventory or pricing incorrectly.  
- Above 70% for long stretches? You're underpricing and giving away money.

A 50% STR means you’re balanced and not stagnant, not frantic.

---

## **3. Post $300–$350 in Listings Every Single Day**

This is the real engine of growth on eBay.

You should be listing **$300–$350 worth of inventory per day**, based on the *listing price*, not your cost or expected sale price.

For most people, that’s:

**6–7 listings per day.**

That’s it.  
But you have to do it every day.

Listing consistently performs better than listing in big bursts. eBay rewards daily activity. Steady effort over sporadic sprints.

---

### **Handling a Backlog (The Right Way)**

If you’ve piled up too much inventory:

1. Count out 14 days of listings totaling $300–$350 per day.  
2. Commit to listing that amount every day for two weeks.  
3. **Stop sourcing entirely** until you catch up.

This frees up cashflow, clears mental clutter, and forces your business back into alignment.

Most sellers don’t have a sourcing problem, they have a listing problem.

---

## **4. Consistency Wins Everything**

If you want eBay to work, you have to treat it like brushing your teeth, not like a weekend project.

List every day.  
Even if it’s only a few items.  
Even on the days you don’t feel like it.

Sellers who grow are the ones who show up.

---

## **5. Offer Free Shipping (Always)**

People love to debate this, but here’s the truth:

**Free shipping sells better on eBay. Period.**

Buyers sort by it. They filter by it. They click it.  
So you should bake the shipping cost into your price.

**Example pricing model:**

- **Item cost:** $12  
- **Shipping materials:** $1.50  
- **Shipping label:** $5.50  

Your total cost = **$19.00**

Add your target 40% profit:

- 40% of $19 = **$7.60**

Your listing price becomes:

**$26.60 → round to $27–$28 (Free Shipping)**

This keeps your margins healthy and avoids the race to the bottom.

---

# **Final Thoughts**

If you follow these rules:

1. **Keep your ASP around $50**  
2. **Aim for a 50% Sell-Through Rate**  
3. **List $300–$350 per day**  
4. **Offer free shipping and price for margin**  

you will build a consistent, profitable eBay business.

This isn’t about hustling harder.  
It’s about sticking to a simple system.

And it works.

## **Bonus: Let’s Talk About eBay Promotions (And Why I’m Testing *Not* Using Them)**

Everyone wants a definitive answer on whether you should use eBay’s built in promotions.  
The truth? **I don’t have one yet and that’s important to say out loud.**

Right now, I’m testing something a lot of sellers don’t talk about:

**Running my entire store *without* eBay Promotions.**

Here’s why:

- Promotions cut into your margins  
- They inflate operating costs  
- Once they’re on, sellers feel pressured to keep raising the rate  
- And I genuinely think strong fundamentals may outperform promotions entirely

But again this is an experiment.

I’m running my system cleanly (high ASP, strong STR, daily listings, free shipping) and seeing whether that alone can drive consistent sales.

I don’t know the final verdict yet.  
And that’s okay.

That’s part of building a real business: you test things, gather data, and adjust based on what actually works, not what a reseller Facebook group insists is gospel.

When I have real numbers, I’ll share the results.  
Until then, take this as permission to question everything and run your own experiments.
